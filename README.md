<h2 id="overview">Overview</h2>

<p>
  <strong>Space Racer</strong> is a compact, high-energy arcade racing game built around short races,
  readable rival behavior, and that satisfying feeling of threading your way through danger at speed.
  You pick your ride, launch from the grid, survive the countdown, and fight your way through a five-vehicle pack.
</p>

<p>
  The tone is intentionally playful rather than ultra-serious. Each vehicle has its own personality, the track is
  built to keep pressure on you, and the whole experience aims to feel like a quick blast of momentum instead of a
  long simulation session.
</p>

<blockquote>
  <p>
    Think: colorful machines, tight racing lines, slipstream mind games, and just enough bumping to make every
    overtake feel earned.
  </p>
</blockquote>

<h2 id="game-mechanics">Game Mechanics</h2>

<h3>Vehicle Selection</h3>

<p>
  Before the race, you browse a roster of distinct vehicles and choose the one you want to pilot. The selection
  screen is part showroom, part mood-setter: it is there to help you commit to a ride that feels right before the
  lights go out.
</p>

<h3>Arcade Speed Over Simulation</h3>

<p>
  The handling aims for immediacy. You are not wrestling with a heavy sim-style ruleset here. The fun comes from
  responding quickly, carrying momentum, and staying composed while the race gets noisy around you.
</p>

<h3>Drafting</h3>

<p>
  Driving through another vehicle's trail is not just visual flair. It can help you build a speed advantage, so
  following the right rival at the right moment can turn defense into attack. If you stay in the wake long enough,
  the payoff becomes noticeable.
</p>

<h3>Collisions Matter</h3>

<p>
  Vehicles do not simply ghost through each other. Contact has consequences: bad angles can slow you down, disrupt
  your line, or hand momentum to somebody else. That gives close racing a little bite and makes traffic management
  part of the challenge.
</p>

<h3>Readable Rivals</h3>

<p>
  Opponents are meant to be readable at a glance. Their presence in the race is not background decoration; they are
  part of the pacing. You should be able to spot pressure, react to position changes, and make quick decisions while
  the pack reshuffles.
</p>

<h3>Short, Replayable Races</h3>

<p>
  The structure encourages another run. Pick a different vehicle, attack the opening lap more aggressively, use the
  draft better, avoid one costly hit, and the whole race can feel different.
</p>

<details>
  <summary><strong>What usually decides a strong run?</strong></summary>
  <ul>
    <li>A clean launch when the countdown ends.</li>
    <li>Using the pack instead of fearing it.</li>
    <li>Taking draft opportunities without getting trapped.</li>
    <li>Avoiding clumsy side contact in crowded moments.</li>
    <li>Keeping enough control to finish lap two without panic.</li>
  </ul>
</details>

<h2>Final Note</h2>

<p>
  Space Racer works best when you play it a little boldly. The race is short, the field is active, and hesitation
  costs more than confidence. Pick a machine you like, trust your line, and go make lap two messy for everyone else.
</p>
